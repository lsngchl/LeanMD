# Radial and Tangential Eigenvalues of a Radial Hessian

For \(z\in\mathbb R^d\setminus\{0\}\), put \(u=z/|z|\).

The decomposition
\[
\mathbb R^d=\mathbb Ru\oplus u^\perp
\]
separates the radial direction \(\mathbb Ru\) from the tangential directions \(u^\perp\).

Since \(zz^Tu=|z|^2u\) and \(zz^Tv=0\) for \(v\in u^\perp\),
\[
\begin{aligned}
A_a(z)u&=a(a-1)|z|^{a-2}u,\\
A_a(z)v&=a|z|^{a-2}v \qquad (v\in u^\perp).
\end{aligned}
\]
Thus the radial eigenvalue is \(a(a-1)|z|^{a-2}\), and every tangential eigenvalue is \(a|z|^{a-2}\).

When \(d=1\), there are no nonzero tangential directions.
